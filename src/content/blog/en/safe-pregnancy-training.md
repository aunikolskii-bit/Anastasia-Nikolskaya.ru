## Why format matters

During pregnancy, your body goes through significant changes — and the load that felt normal before may no longer be appropriate or safe. That is why the format of training matters as much as the training itself: who leads it, how the program is built, and whether it accounts for your stage and how you feel.

## What to look for

### Personal approach

Safe pregnancy training is not a group class where everyone does the same thing. A good specialist considers your specific term, fitness level, and how you feel day to day.

### Calm progression

A solid approach starts with a gentle entry into load. There is no need to "catch up" or prove your fitness. Load should increase gradually and only when you feel well.

### Awareness of limitations

A qualified specialist knows the difference between normal training discomfort and signals that call for stopping or adjusting. When medical restrictions exist, it is important to work within the trainer's scope and recommend consulting a physician when needed.

## What to avoid

- Group classes with no pregnancy adaptation
- Programs with sharp movements, jumps, or high intensity
- Specialists who do not ask about your condition
- Promises of "guaranteed results" or "fast recovery"
