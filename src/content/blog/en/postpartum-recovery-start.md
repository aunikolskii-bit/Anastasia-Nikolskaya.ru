## Why a gentle start matters

After childbirth, your body needs time to recover. This applies not only to the physical state, but also to the emotional one: fatigue, a new rhythm of life, and a changed sense of your own body. Trying to "get back in shape" too quickly often leads to the opposite effect.

## When can you start

There is no universal answer. Much depends on how the birth went, how you feel, and what your physician recommended. For some, it is 6 weeks. For others, it may be several months.

Important: the decision to begin training is best made together with your doctor, especially if there were complications or a C-section.

## Where to start

### Breathing and body awareness

The first step is not load, but reconnecting with your body. Breathing practices, gentle core activation, and pelvic floor awareness form the foundation for everything that follows.

### No comparisons

Every postpartum journey is individual. Comparing yourself to others or to your "previous shape" leads to frustration. What matters more is understanding what your body needs right now.

### Gradual progression

Increasing load should be smooth and based on how you feel, not on a fixed schedule. A good specialist helps build a route, not push toward a result.
